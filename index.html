1. Jacksonville Jaguars

Trevor Lawrence- QB, Clemson

Jacksonville has already done a lot in free agency to make them competetive this upcoming season. But with the first pick ine the 2021 NFL draft, it has been quite apparent for a while who they are taking. Lawrence has been the overwhelming favorite to be the first pick for several years now, and it was more of a question of who was going to suck to get him. The answer turned out to be the Jaguars. And with some lean years seemling since the Franchise started, Jaguar fans now have a franshise QB that they can call theirs for years to come.

2. New York Jets

Zach Wilson- QB, BYU

This pick has been a litte more controverseal with QB being the concensis position, but which player being more of the question. Zach Wilson has emerged as the favorite to go second overall. With his electric style of play and big play potential people have fallen in love with Wilson. There have even been comparisons to Patrick Mahomes in regards to keeping plays alive and the threat of scoring on any play.This does mean they will have to figure out what they are doing with Sam Darnold, but that can be done at the draft or before to aquire more draft capitol. If they are going surprise to ride with Darnold, it would be no surprise if they took their pick of the offensive lineman her at number 2, or traded down to add more first round picks as they continue to rebuild

3. Miami Dolphins

Ja'Marr Chase- WR, LSU

The Dolphins have a huge choise with their current QB Tua and whether or not he is the future. If they deside to keep him as the long term solution, Chase will add a playmaker on the outside that they desperetly need. They have a good wide recieving core, but no one that scares you. This would give the offesne another dimension and give Tua another weapon to help his growth. Even if the rumors are true and they do land Deshawn Watson, they need a playmaker for him to throw to. There is a big chance they draft one of the top quarterback availabel, or draft one of the top offensive lineman with the number three overall pick as well. But they have spent the last couple of years trying to retool the offensive line, and I think they can address that with a later pick.

4. Atlanta Falcons

Justin Fields- QB, Ohio State

This will also be a simple pick for the Falcons I believe. They will take their choice of the quarterbacks left. If the Jets decide to go with Darnold and go away from Zach Wilson, and no one jumps up to take him, the Falcons may still go with the bigger athlete in Justin Fields or Trey Lance. These two are more of a project, but they are looking for someone to learn under Matt Ryan for a season or two.

5. Cincinnati Bengals

Penei Sewell- OT, Oregon

Offensive Tackle is never a sexy pick, but I think that every single Bengals fan will admit that they need to protect their 2020 first round pick Joe Burrow. Burrow made the most of his time at quarterback for the Bengals before his season ending knee injury. It seemed on every play that Burrow was running for his life or getting hit in the backfield. Sewell is one of the best offensive tackle prospects in a while, and Cincinnati would be stupid for not taking him with the fith pick (if he is there). There are some other great prospects at the tackle position that if Sewell is not there, they can easily pivot to, but Sewell would definitely be the apple of the Bengles eye.

6. Philadelphia Eagles

Kyle Pitts- TE, Florida

Pitts come in as easily the best TE poroscpect in this draft, but also arguably the top pass catching target of this draft class. Zach Ertz made a great career in Philadelphia, and got his ring, but the Eagles are not expected to bring him back this year and their other tight end in Dallas Goedert being a free agent after this season, they will need to reload at this position. The Eagles are also just revamping their entire pass catching core, and Pitts will be a great building block for young quarterback Jalen Hurts. Do not be surprised if they do look wide reciever here as well.

7. Detroit Lions

Jaylen Waddle- WR, Alabama

Detroit made their move in trading away their best quarterback in franchise history in Matthew Stafford this offseason, signifying they might be taking a step back and developing a new core. This was also seen with thier decision in not franchise tagging Kenny Galladay, and letting him walk. They also lost Marvin Jones in free agency. The Lions I would think are looking to retool and get yonger on the edge and give their new quarterback Jarred Goff a playmaker.

8. Carolina Panthers

Trey Lance- QB, North Dekota State

The Panthers have been in on just about any quarterback that has been rumored wanting out of their current team, or teams possibly moving on. If they do not land one of the top names on that POTENTIAL trade black. They will most likely draft the Teddy Bridgewater's heir apparent. They have been heavily in scouting all the top quarterbacks of this draft and it would be a big surprise if they tried trading up to get thir quarterback that they want the most. They have been heavily linked to Trey Lance, despite only being a one year starter and starting the Bison's only game this year. He is a big time prospect but still pretty raw. I think a perfect candidate to learn under a great mentor quarterback like Bridgewater.

9. Denver Broncos

Micah Parsons- LB, Penn State

The Broncos can go in so many directions, and it all starts with thier quarterback, Drew Lock. They have at times come out as saying they think he is a franchise quarterback, and otheres offered him as trade bait to land a top quarterback on the POTENTIAL market. I have them taking one of the most athletic linbackers that in this draft in Parsons. He would dramatically help this defense that is already pretty solid. With the signing of Fuller, their cornerback position has a better feel to it, so I think a corner this high for them is not the best option, but a trade down may also be in play as well. But Parson will be a great linebacker for them or whoever is able to get him.

10. Dallas Cowboys

Rashawn Slater- OT, Northwestern

I think the Cowboys missed out on solitifying the future of the offensive line last year, when they took CeeDee Lamb at wide reciever instead. And it is no guarantee that Jerry Jones will not look to make another splash pick with their top 10 pick. But they SHOULD look to bolster their offensive line to protect their newley high paid quarterback in Dak Prescott, who is coming off a year where he broke his ankle early in the year. Again offensive line may not be a sexy pick but Slater is a solid pick and could be a cornerstone of their line for years to come. Jerry should swallow his pride and go ahead and do the sensible thing and draft a lineman and get back to the best offensive line in football.
